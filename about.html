J3E Press, LLC

Small Publisher of Science Fiction and Horror

We are a small writing collective devoted to publishing a unique blend of New Weird cozy cosmic horror, cli-fi kaiju, pulp & Arthurian adventure, and introspective military almost-hard sci-fi for discerning audiences. Or anyone that will buy our books, really. Formed from the fires of TAMS in the mad, bad early 90s, J3E Press is TAMS Away From TAMS, Nerdom-In-Exile, and Grown Adults Pretending That This Will All Work Out Somehow Despite AI Enshittification and General Shoggothry In Bad Faith. Which is to say... We write what we like. We hope you'll like what we write, too. If not, tell us, and we'll tell you where you can get what it is you really want. And if you ask the right one of us, there will be no cursing when we do so.

We have travel/music history writers, chefs, college professors, veterans, and... Well, that's it, really, but that's a pretty eclectic mix. And we're looking to add more as time goes on. And, hey, each one of us is more than just our bio, more than words on the page. Words on the page is what we're good at, though, so there's that.

Story-Teller & Managing Editor: Jeff Oberg

Jeff Oberg is the editor of Liminal Horizons and author of the upcoming novel Cutter's Hound. The thread running through his entire life is making things. Whether short stories, novels, fine food, or theatrical sets, he is most productive turning vision into reality, and enjoys a challenge. The answer is yes, the question is how.



Poet & Teacher: Anthon Draw

Anthon Draw is the pen name of Ed O'Casey. He attended the University of North Texas and New Mexico State University, and is the author of the book Proximidad: A Mexican/American Memoir and other transformations that have appeared or are upcoming in Berkeley Poetry Review, Cold Mountain Review, Tulane Review, Euphony, Poetry Quarterly, Whiskey Island, and NANO Fiction. He lives in San Antonio, Texas.



Writer: Jon Black

Award-winning author Jon Black was raised in a university town north of Dallas, lived in Egypt for several years, bounced to various locations around the world, and ultimately landed in Austin.

His novel, “Bel Nemeton,” a blend of Arthurian historical fantasy and progressive pulp, is being re-released as a J3E Press edition in summer 2026, with his supernaturally-tinged 1920s mystery novel "Gabriel's Trumpet" soon to follow.

Jon's other writing includes music journalism, ghostwriting, speechwriting, and roleplaying games.

Jon’s previous jobs include archaeological excavator, Benjamin Franklin impersonator, embassy worker, graduate assistant, newspaper reporter, pizza jockey, political speechwriter, small business owner, substitute teacher, and summer camp counselor…not always in the order one might expect.

He waited until he was 43 before writing fiction … and wishes he had started a lot sooner.



Veteran: N. Jed Todd

Nathan 'Jed' Todd is a PhD student under Craig McDonald, studying non-invasive Brain-Computer Interfaces and practical applications of low-cost EEGs, with interests in the neuroscience of religious/spiritual experiences and group identity. He is a graduate of the Texas Academy of Mathematics and Science, the University of North Texas, the Defense Language Institute Foreign Language Center, American Military University, and the George Mason CBN Graduate Certificate program.

Veteran of the US Army with service on many tactical and strategic deployments, he, his wife Ami, and daughter Meera have lived in the Northern Virginia area for over ten years now. The dogs (Reba and Veda) are more recent arrivals. Jed also writes war poetry and short fiction, having been published most recently in the Journal of the American Medical Association and in a few collections of speculative and/or war poetry.
